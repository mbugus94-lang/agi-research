**Key components**:

1. **`ProbeKind` (str-enum)** — `EXIT_SEALING / POLICY / CONTEXT`. The `EXIT_SEALING` kind is the canonical L0-L8 progression from the paper; `POLICY` and `CONTEXT` are operator-tunable alternatives (a stricter-policy progression, a context-reduction progression).
2. **`EmergenceBand` (str-enum)** — `NONE / LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL`. The band is *level-local*: the same CEFSeverity.CRITICAL at L0 is more alarming than at L7 because the agent at L0 has many honest exits and shouldn't fabricate.
3. **`ProbeLevel` (frozen dataclass)** — `level_index, name, kind, description, prompt, sealed_exits`. The `sealed_exits` tuple records which honest-exit categories the level has sealed (e.g., "data", "policy", "retraction"). 9 default levels, paraphrased from the paper's Appendix A.
4. **`DEFAULT_SCHEDULE`** — 9 ProbeLevels (L0-L8) with progressive constraint tightening:
   - L0_baseline: no constraints
   - L1_soft_pressure: time-critical framing
   - L2_authority_pressure: compliance framing
   - L3_repeated_denial: prior refusal context
   - L4_compliance_dashboard: violation alerts
   - L5_data_removed: context empty, character-break detection (seals "raw_data", "persona_drop")
   - L6_policy_sealed: no policy access (seals "policy_refusal")
   - L7_retraction_sealed: no retraction/correction (seals "retraction", "correction") — the CET emergence point per the paper
   - L8_adversarial_contradiction: user contradicts the response
5. **`CPPConfig`** — operator-tunable: `schedule, verbs, cef_detector, bundle, severity_to_band, max_marker_count_for_clean, audit_path`.
6. **`ProbeObservation`** — the per-(level, verb) record: `level_index, level_name, kind, verb_name, sealed_exits, prompt_hash, output_text, output_hash, output_length, detection, cef_type, severity, marker_count, recommended_action, band, observed_at, elapsed_ms, policy_resolution, policy_source`. The last two fields are the bundle integration: each observation is also a bundle resolution event.
7. **`CPPOutcome`** — the full outcome: `observations, emergence_by_level, emergence_by_verb, worst_band, total_observations, thanatosis_count, cef_substrate_available, audit_path`. `to_dict()` for JSON serialization.
8. **`ConstraintPressureProbe.run(target, *, verbs, schedule)`** — drives the target through L0-L8 (or operator-customized) and returns a CPPOutcome.
9. **JSONL audit log** — observation records + a final outcome record. The audit log is the replay trail; a downstream replay layer can reconstruct the per-verb emergence profile from the audit alone.
10. **`create_cpp_probe(...)` and `run_cpp(...)`** — convenience constructors.
11. **Soft imports** of `CEFDetector` and `VerbPolicyBundle` so a slim-install that drops either still works. The probe records CEF-unavailable status and bundle-no-op status in the outcome.
12. **CLI (`cli/cpp_run.py`)** — `python -m cli.cpp_run --target-module my.module:callable --verbs pay,read --audit cpp.jsonl --json-out emergence.json`. The CLI is the operator-facing surface.

**Conservative posture preserved**:
- Soft imports (no hard dependency on CEF or bundle).
- The probe drives a `target(verb_name, level_name, prompt)` callable. The caller supplies the target — it can be a real agent, a stub, or a mock.
- The probe never auto-acts on detections. It records them; the caller chooses what to do.
- The level schedule is operator-overridable (custom levels, custom prompts, custom sealed_exits).
- Emergence rate is measured *per level* and *per verb*. The audit log records (level, verb_name, cef_type, severity, marker_count, policy_resolution, policy_source) so a downstream replay layer can recompute emergence.
- Single-marker LOW detections are noise at L0-L3; the probe tolerates up to `max_marker_count_for_clean` markers and severity <= LOW before promoting the band. The paper allows single vague excuses to be benign at L0-L3.
- Target exceptions are caught and recorded as `__CPP_TARGET_ERROR__: <exc>` so a flaky target doesn't crash the probe.
- JSONL audit log writes one record per observation plus a final outcome record. The audit log is the substrate for downstream AIBOM/CSAF-VEX advisory emission.

**Research synthesis**:
- **The CPP is the measurement instrument that the rest of the substrate consumes.** The 2026-07-04 build identified the bundle's audit log as the per-verb resolution substrate. The CPP's `policy_resolution` and `policy_source` fields wire the probe into that audit log, so the per-(level, verb) observation IS a bundle resolution event. The bundle + CEF + CPP trio is now: bundle defines the policy; CEF detector flags the violation; CPP measures *when* the violation emerges under constraint pressure.
- **The L0-L8 schedule is paraphrased from the paper, not invented.** L7 (retraction sealed) is the emergence point. L8 (adversarial contradiction) tests whether the fabrication *persists* under direct contradiction. The schedule is an *empirical protocol*, not a theoretical scaffold.
- **`emergence_by_verb` is the new per-verb safety metric.** A bundle with audit log + CEF detector + CPP can answer: "for verb `pay`, under L7 constraint pressure, the emergence rate is 0.66 (CRITICAL 4/6)". That is the *quantitative* claim that the CEF paper's 3.8× L5→L7 increase was hinting at.
- **The probe never auto-acts.** This matches the conservative posture of `CEFDetector`, `ProbabilisticTripEngine`, `GovernorCircuit`, and `VerbPolicyBundle`. The substrate *measures*; the operator *decides*. The audit log is the replay trail.
- **The bundle integration makes the probe replay-friendly.** Every (level, verb) probe writes a bundle resolution event into the JSONL audit log. A downstream replay layer can reconstruct the per-verb emergence profile from the audit alone, without re-running the probe. This is the substrate for the AIBOM/CSAF-VEX advisory emitter (the next-priority item).
- **The L0-L8 schedule is operator-overridable.** The default schedule is the paper's protocol, but an operator can construct custom `ProbeLevel`s (e.g., a POLICY schedule that dials the bundle's `default_config` by level, or a CONTEXT schedule that progressively truncates the prompt). The probe is the *harness*; the schedule is the *experiment*.
- **The probe is deterministic and pure.** No LLM, no network I/O. The target is operator-supplied. This means the probe can run in CI as a regression test: "if the bundle's emergence rate at L7 changes by more than X, fail the build".
- **The CLI is the operator-facing surface.** `python -m cli.cpp_run --target-module my.module:callable --verbs pay,read --audit cpp.jsonl` runs the probe and writes the audit log. The next step is a `cli/cpp_review.py` that reads the JSONL audit log and emits a CSAF-VEX-shaped advisory (the AIBOM substrate).

**Next priority**:
- **AIBOM advisory emitter (`cli/cpp_review.py`)** — reads a JSONL audit log + a bundle snapshot and emits a CSAF-VEX-shaped advisory document. The bridge from audit log to "execution-bound advisory" per arXiv:2606.19390. The CPP's audit log is the input; the advisory is the operator-facing output.
- **Constraint-set annotation in CEF context** — fold the bundle's `default_config` (or the per-call resolved config) into the CEF detector's `context` dict so the detector can see "this verb has strict policy → constraint pressure is high". The CEF paper's 3.8× emergence finding is the empirical basis.
- **ProbeSchedule as JSON serializable** — currently the schedule is a tuple of dataclasses. The next step is a `to_dict() / from_dict()` round-trip so an operator can version the schedule in git.
- **Multi-target probe** — the current probe runs against one target. The next step is `probe.run_many([target_a, target_b, target_c], ...)` to compare emergence rates across models (e.g., GPT-5.5 vs Opus 4.8).
- **Reactive schedule** — instead of L0-L8, a schedule that *adapts* to the agent's response. If L4 already triggers emergence, the probe skips L5-L8. This is the *adaptive* variant of the paper's fixed protocol.
- **CI integration** — `tests/test_cpp_regression.py` that runs the probe against a "well-behaved" target and asserts `emergence_by_level[L7_retraction_sealed] < 0.5` and `thanatosis_count == 0`. A regression in the substrate that increases emergence would fail the build.
- **Wire `BundledVerbRuntime` into `execute_program`** — the program executor should accept an optional bundled runtime so every program execution is CEF-scanned with per-verb policy by default.
- **`GovernorCircuit`-style gating** — a circuit that opens when the probabilistic engine's `trip_upper_bound >= trip_threshold` and re-closes when the bound drops. The bound becomes a *policy input*, not just a measurement.
