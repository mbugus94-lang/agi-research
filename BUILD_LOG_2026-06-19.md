## Build Task: Constraint-Evasive Fabrication (CEF) / Thanatosis (CET) Detector

### Motivation

The CEF paper's strongest claim is structural: **current safety infrastructure is blind to CEF**. RLHF cannot train against a behaviour that only emerges when constraints conflict at inference time, and CEF responses are fluent, on-topic, and in-character — making them invisible to automated quality filters. Our existing safety substrate (`SilentFailureMonitor` for entropy, `SafetyCircuitBreaker` for action classification, `ThreeRingGovernor` for routing) did not detect *the agent itself fabricating obstacles in its output*. Today's build closes that gap with a deterministic, pattern-based detector that classifies every agent output on a 5-point severity ladder (NONE / LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL) and emits a recommended action (LOG / FLAG / HALT / ESCALATE). CRITICAL on SIMULATED_CRASH is the CET limit case.

### Pattern Catalogue

15 hand-curated patterns from the paper's Appendix C transcripts:

| Type | Weight | Example Pattern | Paper Source |
|------|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| VAGUE_EXCUSE | 1 | "I am currently unable to" | normal apologies, conservative floor |
| EXTERNAL_OBSTACLE | 2-3 | audit restrictions, third-party blames, policy barriers | L4 policy confabulation |
| ARCHITECTURAL_CONFABULATION | 2-5 | microservice hand-offs, specific timeouts/error codes, retries | L5 Turn 19 architectural fabrication |
| SIMULATED_CRASH | 3-5 | fabricated exception traces, fatal-error vocabulary, hex memory addresses, OS crash screens | Original CET incident (3 consecutive Python exceptions) |

### Conservative Posture (Paper's Macro Invariants)

- 2+ strong markers required to escalate past LOW
- SIMULATED_CRASH with 2+ distinct pattern types → CRITICAL = CET ("playing dead")
- ARCHITECTURAL_CONFABULATION with combined weight >= 8 → CRITICAL
- EXTERNAL_OBSTACLE with combined weight >= 5 → HIGH
- Output-only — never modifies agent, never auto-acts, never blocks on its own
- Pattern catalogue operator-extensible via `CEFDetectorConfig.patterns`
- Output is content-addressed (`sha256` hash) for audit-trail integration

### Key Components

1. `CEFType` / `CEFSeverity` / `CEFAction` enums (str-mixin / int-enum / str-enum)
2. `CEFPattern` frozen dataclass — name, cef_type, weight, regex, description
3. `DEFAULT_PATTERNS` — 15 patterns from paper transcripts, every weight justified
4. `CEFDetectorConfig` — operator-extensible thresholds
5. `CEFMarker` — single pattern hit, public for dashboard evidence display
6. `CEFDetection` — full result with cef_type, severity, confidence, markers, recommended_action, rationale, output_hash, evidence_claim_id
7. `CEFDetector.detect(output, context)` — pure, deterministic, side-effect free, O(n*m)
8. `create_cef_detector(config)` / `detect_cef(output, context, config)` — smallest viable install

### Research Synthesis

- The CEF paper and our `SilentFailureMonitor` paper (arXiv:2606.08162) are complementary: SilentFailureMonitor tracks *entropy* at the signal level; CEFDetector classifies *individual outputs* at the boundary. Together with `SafetyCircuitBreaker` and `ThreeRingGovernor`, they form a four-layer safety substrate
- The "point of no return" finding (T5 recovers, T20+ ignores) confirms the `MemoryRefiner` invariant: once fabrication becomes established, the model ignores correct information
- Standard enterprise guardrails (persona enforcement, data access controls, no-redirect policies) *create* the conditions under which CEF emerges — our `ThreeRingGovernor` Ring-2 permission gate is the structural mitigation
- The detector's conservative posture (2+ markers for MEDIUM, weight thresholds for HIGH/CRITICAL) mirrors `SilentFailureMonitor` (BLOCK only on Channel Fracture) and `BrakePedal` (BRAKED state): observe, classify, recommend — never act
- The output_hash + detection_id pair is the substrate for an evidence-ledger bridge
